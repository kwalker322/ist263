Breakfast Recipes

Oatmeal

There is nothing more comfortable and as filling as good old-fashioned oatmeal. This Vegan

breakfast can be made on the stove or in the microwave and takes less than 5-minutes.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 2 cups vegan milk (unsweetened almond milk is recommended)
  • 1 medium banana, mashed
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinamon
  • Pinch of salt

Directions:

Combine all the ingredients into a small saucepan and turn heat to

medium/high. Bring the oatmeal to a boil, turn the heat down to

low/medium, and continually stir for sound 3-5 minutes. Once the

oatmeal thickens and is at your desired consistency, serve and enjoy!

Vegan Potato Hash

A delicious and filling breakfast that I love to make when I have leftover potatoes!

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 pounds of potatoes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/4 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 red onion, sliced
  • 8 ounces extra-firm tofu, drained and pressed. (Trader Joes has pre-drained and pressed tofu that is perfect for college students)
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 large bundle of kale (I like to get the pre-washed and cut kale to use in my dorm)

Directions:

Preheat your oven to 400 degrees Fahrenheit. Cut the potatoes in half and toss with 1 tablespoon olive oil, paprika, cumin, and ½ teaspoon salt. Place on a baking sheet and roast for 25-30 minutes until crispy.

While the potatoes are cooking, heat the remaining tablespoon of oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the red onion and cook until softened (about 5 minutes). Crumble in the tofu, turmeric, making sure to break down the tofu into small pieces as you toss it until it's crispy and is darker in color (about 5 minutes). Add in the kale and cook another 2-3 minutes until kale is wilted.

When serving, add ½ avocado to each bowl and add any desired sauce. Enjoy!

Vegan Banana Oatmeal Pancakes

2-step pancake recipe that only takes 10 min!

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup oats
  • 1/2 cup almond milk (or any form of dairy-free milk)
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 1 banana
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla essence
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • Coconut oil, for cooking
  • Maple syrup and your favorite topping (fruit, nut butter, jam, etc.)

Directions:

Place all the ingredients in a blender (except the coconut oil) and process until completely smooth.

Drizzle the coconut oil in a large skillet and add the pancake batter until the edges are entirely brown.

Flip the pancake and cook for another 2-3 minutes. Serve with some more maple syrup and another

topping your heart desires!
